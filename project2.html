Architecture and Color

Program: Illustrator

As part of a Design Fundamentals class, the aim of this project was to recreate photos of the San Francisco architecture as illustrations, using these photos as a template. The purpose was to learn how to work in shapes to create form instead of relying on lines. In a total of nine different compositions, each row represents a different facet of a building: the top row is for roofs, the middle row shows the sides of buildings, and the bottom row is for doors. Each of these sections were meant to showcase different aspects of one’s life, with the sky showing endless possibility for each of them. The color palette was cast from a pistachio: with two different shades of mauve/purple and three opposing shades of green, the form of each building throughout the compositions were brought about.
